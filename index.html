HOW TO USE
EMULATION DEVICE

Enter the screen resolution of the device you are emulating on (width × height in pixels).

EMULATED CONSOLE

Select the console whose output you want to visualize. Consoles with CRT correction will offer a toggle for 4:3 aspect ratio — as they appeared on CRT televisions.

INTEGER SCALING

Scales the console output by whole numbers only (2×, 3×, 4×...). Preserves pixel sharpness. Without integer scaling, the maximum possible fill is used, which may result in a pixel imbalance (f.e. visible in Megaman games, spec. a healthbar) and will require pixe interpolation shader to alleviate it.

OVERSCALE PREVIEW

Shows what would happen at one integer step higher than what fits. The part of the console output that exceeds the screen is highlighted in red. The INFO panel shows how many pixels would be cut — both at screen resolution (CUT W/H) and at the console's native resolution (CUT NATIVE).

INFO PANEL

SCALE — the resulting scale factor. Shown in red if the console exceeds the device.
OUTPUT — the final resolution of the console output on screen.
COVERAGE — percentage of the screen area filled by the console output.
BARS W/H — unused pixels on the sides / top and bottom.
